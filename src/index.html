UChicago Medicine

My team and I worked with UChicago Medicine to address a major operational issue: nearly 20% of scheduled appointments resulted in no-shows or cancellations. After analyzing patient data and collaborating with staff, we identified key gaps in communication and engagement across specific demographics. We developed a solution that included automated email workflows, targeted surveys, and incentive-based strategies to improve attendance. This project strengthened my ability to connect data analysis with real operational changes that directly impact patient outcomes.

ReMade In Cambodia

I led market research for ReMade In Cambodia as part of a team project focused on supporting their entry into the U.S. market. We started by designing and distributing surveys to target consumers, then used regression analysis and t-tests to better understand how American audiences perceive Cambodian brands. Our goal wasn’t just to analyze the data, but to turn it into something actionable. Based on our findings, we developed strategic recommendations around branding, positioning, and market entry, which we ultimately presented directly to the company’s CEO. This experience helped me get more comfortable working with ambiguous problems and reinforced the importance of communicating data clearly to drive real business decisions.

U.S. Chronic Disease Data & Representation

In this project, I analyzed two large U.S. chronic disease datasets using Tableau to explore how well different populations are represented in disease research. What stood out most was a significant imbalance in the data. This research was overwhelmingly concentrated on white patient populations, in some cases at rates nearly seven times higher than other groups. I also identified that diseases like COPD, cardiovascular disease, and cancer received the most attention across datasets. This project pushed me to think more critically about the limitations of data, not just the results. It reinforced the idea that how data is collected can be just as important as what it shows, especially in healthcare settings where representation directly impacts outcomes.
